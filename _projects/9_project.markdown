### Deep Genomics — AI-Enabled Target Discovery

As part of the AI-Enabled Target Discovery group at Deep Genomics, I have contributed to the development and deployment of three large-scale neural network models for RNA biology.

**BigRNA** is an RNA foundation model trained on diverse transcriptomic data to learn generalizable representations of gene expression and RNA processing. It enables discovery of disease mechanisms and identification of candidate therapeutic targets from sequence alone. The model and its applications are described in [Celaj et al. (2023)](https://doi.org/10.1101/2023.09.20.558508).

**REPRESS** is a sequence-based model for predicting cell-type-specific microRNA binding and mRNA degradation. It is designed for therapeutic discovery, enabling identification of RNA targets across disease-relevant cell types. Described in [Kanuparthi et al. (2025)](https://doi.org/10.1101/2025.05.15.654105).

**Enigma** is an efficient model for deciphering regulatory genomics, designed to scale sequence-based regulatory prediction across the genome. Described in [Jung et al. (2025)](https://doi.org/10.64898/2025.12.18.694875).

My contributions span model benchmarking and evaluation, large-scale inference infrastructure on GCP (GPU/TPU), and productionizing these models as internal tools for experimental scientists across the organization.
